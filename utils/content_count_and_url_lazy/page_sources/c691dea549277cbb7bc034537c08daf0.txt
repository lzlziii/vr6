    <h1>Paradise</h1>
    <h4><div>Marshall Islands</div><div>Pacific Ocean</div></h4>
  </div>
  <p>Ever wondered what a nuclear explosion would feel like first-hand?</p>
  <p>The Finnish Broadcasting Company Yle transports you straight to the heart of the Pacific Ocean with the help of virtual reality. </p>
  <p>Be there on a beach in paradise when Ivy Mike is detonated on Enewetak Atoll.</p>
  <p>In addition to the VR experience, below you will find a feature story by reporter Mika Mäkeläinen from his trip with videographer Jani Saikko to the faraway islands.</p>

    <p>The Paradise VR experience has been created from video and photo footage of Enewetak Atoll taken by Yle journalists. The 1952 explosion of Ivy Mike was digitally recreated and modeled to fit the actual environment.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>To obtain the most realistic-feeling and immersive experience, it is best to use highly developed VR viewing equipment. If you have this kind of gear at your disposal, you can <a href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/1016390/Perspectives_Paradise/" target="_blank">download a suitable version of the VR experience here for free</a> (3.0&nbsp;GB). You’ll need a SteamVR-compatible VR headset – for example, HTC Vive, Oculus Rift or Windows Mixed Reality devices – along with a high-power computer.</li>
      <li>Another alternative is to dive into Enewetak’s amazing atoll with <a href="https://youtu.be/ShZGwgFmzoI" target="_blank">this version</a> of the Paradise VR experience for your mobile phone. See the events as a 360˚ video in the YouTube app by turning your phone sideways or by using inexpensive VR viewers. You can also download a video for your mobile phone or computer <a href="https://yleerilliset-a.akamaihd.net/YleUutiset/2019/paradise/Paradise-360-video.mp4" target="_blank">from this link</a> (mp4 file, 600 MB). The only thing you will need is a player that is equipped to play 360˚ media, such as the Android VR Media Player.
      </li><li>Another option is to watch the Paradise VR experience as a 2D video on your computer screen. Use the mouse to move about and explore your environment.</li>
    </ul>

        <h3>Radiant Paradise</h3>        
        <p>Yosko Luke was just a child so she didn’t know to be frightened when the coral reef of her home atoll was blown to smithereens in a hydrogen bomb blast. After she grew to be an adult, she developed thyroid cancer. A fear of residual radiation from U.S. nuclear weapons testing during the Cold War carries on in the people of Enewetak Atoll to this day. But the presence of radioactive plutonium waste on the islands is no longer the population’s biggest concern.</p>
