							<p>What is a CubeSat?</p>						</div>
				</div>
				<div class="elementor-element elementor-element-c74c946 elementor-invisible elementor-widget elementor-widget-text-editor" data-id="c74c946" data-element_type="widget" data-settings="{&quot;_animation&quot;:&quot;fadeInRight&quot;}" data-widget_type="text-editor.default">
				<div class="elementor-widget-container">
							<p>A CubeSat is a type of minisatellite based on a form factor consisting of 10 cm (3.9 in) cubes. CubeSats weigh no more than 2 kg (4.4 lb) per unit and often use commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) components for their electronics and structure.</p><p>CubeSats are placed into orbit by deployers on the International Space Station or launched as secondary payloads on a launch vehicle.</p><p>By August 2021, more than 1,600 CubeSats had been launched.</p>						</div>

				<div class="elementor-widget-container">
							<p>Earth Rising is a video game where you build and assemble panels for a CubeSat and then assemble the CubeSat and launch it into space.</p><p>After we get the design for assembling the CubeSat, we need to solder the corresponding elements and stack them in a certain way inside the Cube frame and put them on the conveyor belt that drives the CubeSat into the rocket that we send into space.</p><p>Only certain chips and elements can be placed on each panel because each one has a different option, such as: batteries that regulate battery consumption, some read temperature data and the like.</p><p>Some chips that are placed on the board must be soldered, and for this a futuristic soldering iron is used, which is used to properly stack the chips.</p><p>Also in the game is a 3D printer on which we can print the boards and frame of the CubeSat.</p>						</div>
