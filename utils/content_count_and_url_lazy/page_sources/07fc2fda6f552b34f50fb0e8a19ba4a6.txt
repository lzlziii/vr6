<h2><a name="etiquette"></a>What's the Anyland behavior etiquette?</h2> 
 
<p>Anyland is proud to have people from all walks of life and all colors of the rainbow. Respect is one of our building blocks. We strive for a peaceful environment for everyone.</p> 

<p>If you use the context laser (top Vive controller button on the thumb side) on people or things, you'll find options such as friending or reporting. You can also lock people out of your area if you want to.</p>
  
<p>When interacting with others, please be respectful. For instance, cursing at them or otherwise showing hate against them, is against the etiquette. Impersonation or multiple accounts to confuse, deceptive creations outside of voluntary challenges, bullying, strong rudeness in speech or drawings, trolling, spamming, picking of fights, personal attacks, spreading rumors about someone, overly aggressive behavior towards others, homophobia, and racism aren't wanted by most and are against the etiquette. You may not reserve area names for future use, or mass create new areas, as everyone should get a chance at the names. 
Please do not publicize videos or screenshots of Anyland with the intention to embarass or ridicule the person being filmed 
(depending on context, you can also let others know you're recording... the more it's not a group situation, the more expectation one would have to privacy). 
</p> 
 
<p>Often, location matters: what people may consider offensive in one area may be accepted, funny and terrific in another more distant location with another group. Try to apply common sense and find the group and location that's right for you and enjoy! Communication and flagging is the basis for how we all self-maintain.</p> 

<p>Please read the terms below for more.</p>

<h2><a name="forumEtiquette"></a>What's the Anyland forum etiquette?</h2> 

<p>The etiquette and terms for forums follow above etiquette and terms â€“ for instance, to always be respectful. 
In addition:</p> 

<ul> 
<li><p>Please use self-descriptive titles. For instance, the title "Suggestion: Feedable Pets" would be used instead of just "Suggestion".</p></li> 

<li><p>It is against the etiquette to post issues specifically related to an account, or one person, on general forums (or really, any other public place). Account-related issues need to be either discussed in person, or if that's not possible, discussed in private with us via email. This helps the privacy of all involved.</p></li> 

<li><p>Please do not share any personal details of others on the board, including their location, their activities, their hobbies, profession, what they do or don't do, what they told you in chat etc. Please do not represent or quote others, but allow them to speak for themselves. Please do not post photos of others. Please do not share what you found out about others on the web or elsewhere. Again, privacy is important.</p></li> 

<li><p>Complaints about other Anyzens should be written in Flag reports, but not a forum. In addition, you can contact us via email for such issues.</p></li> 

<li><p>Please do not bump your own threads (e.g. adding a follow-up to them if they didn't yet yield a reply). Please contact us via email if you feel something needs to be looked at again.</p></li> 

<li><p>If a thread was closed, please do not reopen it under a new title (you can contact us via email if you want to know more about the reasons).</p></li> 

<li><p>Please do not post very personal information about yourself, like your home address, email address, or phone number on a forum.</p></li> 

<li><p> 
Threads posted should be appropriate for the topic of the board, and comments posted should be appropriate for the subject of the thread posted in. On boards with high posting frequency and a bigger audience, please keep comment and thread relevancy high, the message clear, and posting quantity and frequency in proportion.</p></li> 
</ul> 

<p>A big thanks to everyone who participates in forums!</p>

<h2 id="privacy">Privacy Policy</h2>

<p>Your privacy is very important to us, it's what makes for a comfy experience.</p>

<p>What you speak is never persistently saved with us, it's just transmitted (through a cloud service called Photon).
Web pages which you visit when no one's around, or which are attached to you,
are never transmitted to our servers (and even if others around, they're just transmitted temporarily for the syncing need so others
see the same surface display).</p>
<p></p>

<p>To keep your chat privacy guarded, we take a variety of measures:</p>

<ul>
<li>People can't turn fully invisible around you (unless the area explicitly allows it, at which point you will get
a security message informing you of it)</li>
<li>When people shrink to very small sizes in an area, you will get an alert dialog of that</li>
<li>In your Me â†’ "..." settings, you can tick "Unfindable", which means others (even if they friended you)
can't see your current area. This option will also hide your name from <a href="http://in.anyland.com">in.anyland.com</a></li>
</ul>

<p>The Anyland.com website uses Google Analytics as statistics software, which may add cookies.
</p>

<p>Anyland is self-maintainted by all of us. When someone writes a flag report, this report is private only for the Anyland devs to see.
</p>

<p>When you want most privacy, you can set your area to be private. When that happens, only other editors (or temporarily for some minutes, the people you decide to ping)
can join the area and speak with you. To set your area private, choose the area mountain &amp; sun icon â†’ "..." â†’ Private.
</p>

<p>Objects you create and publicly place (and which are still placed somewhere) may appear in universal searches.
To avoid that, you can context-laser a creation and select Tags â†’ Unlisted.
</p>

<p>To fully disable your microphone in Anyland from transmitting anything, use Me â†’ Microphone â†’ Mute Me.
</p>
