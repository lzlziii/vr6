<p class="font_8">f. Sometimes we’ll ask to get your parent or guardian’s consent</p>
<p class="font_8">You’ll see a notification. We might also ask for some details from them, so that we can contact them.</p>
<p class="font_8">Other times we may contact your parent or guardian:</p>
<ul>
<li class="font_8">so you can get notifications from us</li>
<li class="font_8">so we can show you more things we think you’ll like, based on what you do</li>
<li class="font_8">so you can post comments</li>
<li class="font_8">so you can upload your contribution to us</li>
</ul>
<p class="font_8">g. Sometimes we’ll tell the&nbsp;parents about activities of their children<br>
For example, if you’re under 13 and your parent has asked to see every creation you upload, we’ll share it with them before it goes public.</p>
<p class="font_8">h. When we can contact you</p>
<p class="font_8">Sometimes we need to get in touch with you but we’ll always follow these rules.</p>
<p class="font_8">i. It’s safe to use us</p>
<p class="font_8">It’s our job to keep your personal information safe and secure. That’s why we design our services with your safety in mind. And we’re always looking for the best ways to improve this. There are also things that you can do to keep safe, like thinking about what information you share and how to keep your devices secure.</p>
<p class="font_8">j. Human beings and not robots will make the big decisions that affect you</p>
<p class="font_8">k. You’re in control of how we use cookies and tracking</p>
<p class="font_8">Cookies are bits of data that are stored on your device. Some things on our websites wouldn’t be able to work without these cookies. So they’re always on when you visit us. We use cookies and tracking to make things easier for you, like remembering where you got to in a game or what emoji you selected. You can switch off some cookies and similar tracking technologies. Or your parent or guardian can do this if you’re under 13.</p>
<p class="font_8">l. What we do when you post, upload or share a creation</p>
<p class="font_8">When you share your creation with us we’ll try to tell you exactly what we’re going to do with it.</p>
<p class="font_8">m. You’ve got privacy rights, the same as adults, so get to know what they are</p>
<p class="font_8">You can:</p>
<ul>
<li class="font_8">ask us what personal information we hold about you</li>
<li class="font_8">ask us to correct or delete your personal information</li>
<li class="font_8">tell us to stop using your personal information</li>
<li class="font_8">If you’re 13 or older, you can do this yourself, but not always. Sometimes we’ll be asking for your parent or guardian to do that. If you’re under 13 your parent or guardian probably has to do it for you.</li>
</ul>
<h6 class="font_6">11. Can I delete my information?</h6>
<p class="font_8">This depends on what information you’re talking about.</p>
<p class="font_8">a. If you’re talking about Fectar account</p>
<p class="font_8">You can delete your account. Your account information is immediately deleted. Keep in mind:</p>
<ul>
<li class="font_8">we keep a record of how you’ve used our services, but this information can’t be linked back to you</li>
<li class="font_8">we also keep anything you’ve uploaded or commented on</li>
</ul>
<p class="font_8">b. What about other information I’ve shared with you?</p>
<p class="font_8">We might have collected other personal information that you might ask to be deleted that has nothing to do with Fectar account.</p>
<h6 class="font_6">12. What are my rights?</h6>
<p class="font_8">Remember, you’re in control of your personal information.</p>
<p class="font_8">You have the right to:</p>
<ul>
<li class="font_8">request a copy of your information</li>
<li class="font_8">not let robots make big decisions about you</li>
<li class="font_8">to ask us to correct information that’s wrong, to delete it or to request that we only use it for certain purposes</li>
<li class="font_8">to change your mind, and ask us to stop using your information. For example, unsubscribing from any marketing emails or turning off personalisation. Bear in mind, sometimes we might not be able to help. Like if the law tells us we can’t.</li>
</ul>
<h6 class="font_6">13. How does the Fectar use cookies and similar tracking?</h6>
<p class="font_8">a. What are cookies and tracking technologies? Cookies are bits of data which are stored in your computer or mobile when you visit a website or app. There are also similar pieces of tracking information we collect.</p>
<p class="font_8">b. Why do we use cookies and other tracking? To do a few different things:</p>
<ul>
<li class="font_8">to remember information about you, so you don’t have to give it to us again. And again. And again</li>
<li class="font_8">to keep you signed in, even on different devices</li>
<li class="font_8">to help us understand how people are using our services, so we can make them better</li>
<li class="font_8">to deliver advertising to websites</li>
<li class="font_8">to help us personalise Fectar to you by remembering your preferences and settings. And your progress, so you can pause and pick up where you left off watching a space, even on a different device.</li>
<li class="font_8">to find out if our emails have been read and if you find them useful</li>
</ul>
<p class="font_8">A few things on our websites wouldn’t work without some cookies. Tech people call these “strictly necessary cookies”. They’re always on when you visit us. But we want to use others like functional, performance and advertising cookies to make your experience more enjoyable. We’ll only use them if you’ve agreed. You can always change your mind. Bear in mind there are some other cookies out there from other companies. These “third-party cookies” might track how you use different websites, including ours. For example, you might get a social media company’s cookie when you see the option to share something. You can turn them off, but not through us.</p>
<p class="font_8">&nbsp;c. How long do cookies last?</p>
<p class="font_8">Some are erased when you close the browser on your website or app. Others stay longer, sometimes forever, and are saved onto your device so that they’re there when you come back.</p>
<p class="font_8">d. How do I control my cookies and tracking?</p>
<p class="font_8">When you first visit us, we’ll tell you about our cookies and ask you to agree if we can use them. You can always change your mind by going to your settings. Stopping all cookies might mean you can’t access some Fectar services, or that some of them might not work properly for you. Another way to control some tracking is in the settings on your device.​</p>
<h6 class="font_6">14. How will I find out about changes to this policy?</h6>
<p class="font_8">We update this policy sometimes. If we make important changes, like how we use your personal information, we’ll let you know. It might be a notice, an email or a message in your app. If you don’t agree to the changes, then you can always stop using our services, delete your account and stop giving us any more personal information. We’d be sorry to see you go.</p>
