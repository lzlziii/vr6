              <h2>Early-Access</h2>
              <p>We're working very hard to make Volo Airsport a unique, beautiful and thrilling experience, but we're not done yet! With your support we can expand the game, and make it into the best aerial playground you've ever seen.
              </p>
              <p>
                Volo Airsport will change significantly over the course of development. If you are not excited to play the game in its current state, then you may want to wait until the game progresses further in development.
              </p>

              <h3>What does Early Access mean, and what do I get for my purchase?</h3>
              <p>
                You get instant access to the game and can start playing! You get access to current and future versions of Volo Airsport for a <a href="#buy">single purchase</a>.
              </p>
              <p>
                The price will increase as the game grows. If you want to get it cheap, get it now.
              </p>

              <h3>What's in it now?</h3>

              <ul>
                <li>A single, finely tuned wingsuit to fly with</li>
                <li>A fully customizable parachute, with complex aerodynamics</li>
                <li>A huge landscape inspired by the Swiss and French Alps, with lots of lines to try</li>
                <li>An advanced and completely customizable time and weather system</li>
                <li>Time trail courses that show you some cool lines to fly on the map</li>
                <li>Highly configurable graphics and input settings</li>
                <li>VR Support (Gamepad Required)</li>
              </ul>

              <h3>Where is it going?</h3>

              <p>
                The game design is changing as we go along, but we can tell you that we're thinking about the following features:
              </p>
              <ul>
                <li>Online Multiplayer </li>
                <li>Walking, jumping from exit points</li>
                <li>Player-designed Skydiving Styles</li>
                <li>Customizable Gear Design (Aerodynamics &amp; Looks)</li>
                <li>Player-Created Events and Challenges</li>
                <li>Replay Editor</li>
                <li>Additional maps</li>
                <li>Mod Support</li>
              </ul>
          </section>

            <h3 id="system-requirements">System Requirements</h3>

            <p>Volo Airsport is available on Windows, Mac OS X and Linux</p>

            <h4>Minimum System Requirements</h4>

            <ul>
              <li>Windows 7 SP1, Max OS X 10.9, Ubuntu 14.04 LTS</li>
              <li>Dual Core CPU 2.6Ghz</li>
              <li>4GB RAM</li>
              <li>500MB Free Disk Space</li>
              <li>Dedicated Graphics Card (GeForce 650GT or equivalent)</li>
              <li>Broadband Internet Connection (to download updates)</li>
            </ul>

            <p>A dedicated graphics card is definitely recommended. Windows and Mac computers with Intel Integrated Graphics chips: <b>HD4000 minimum</b>, any lower results in very low framerates.</p>

            <h4>VR System Requirements</h4>

            <ul>
              <li>Windows 7 SP1 or up</li>
              <li>Intel i5-4590</li>
              <li>4GB RAM</li>
              <li>NVidia GeForce 970</li>
            </ul>

            <p>
              These requirements are not set in stone. As we refine the game less powerful systems might become able to run the game, especially for VR.
            </p>

            <h4>Supported Controllers</h4>

            <p>
              You can play the game with mouse and/or keyboard, but the game plays best with a gamepad like the Xbox 360, Xbox One or Playstation 4 controllers. Joysticks are also supported.
            </p>

              <h2>Jobs</h2>
              <p>Join the team of two game makers working on the early-access title Volo Airsport.</p>

              <h3>3D artist (Freelance or Internship)</h3>
              <p>We’re looking for someone who can take control over large aspects of the look and feel of the game.
                As a 3D artist you think and discuss about how to best translate the concept art to in-game 3D models.
                Since the game is currently in early-access you will immediately see your work out in the wild.</p>

              <div>The following skills are required:

                <ul>
                  <li>Modeling, texturing, rigging, and to a lesser extent animation.</li>
                  <li>Experience making characters and environment props.</li>
                  <li>Used to working with an iterative workflow.</li>
                  <li>Not afraid to tackle problems you’re not familiar with.</li>
                </ul>

              Plusses would be:

                <ul>
                  <li>Experience with sculpting, retopo, and baking for game engines.</li>
                  <li>Experience with the Unity engine.</li>
                </ul>
              </div>

              <p>You’ll work in a multidisciplinary team where everyone has multiple roles.</p>

              <div>
                We offer:

                <ul>
                <li>A chance to learn the ins-and-outs of developing an early-access game,
                  how to make design decisions, expand the game and deal with feedback from the community.</li>
                <li>A chance to work on an indie title that has a very dedicated community.</li>
                <li>Help with the technicalities that come with building models for a game, including performance and time constraints.</li>
                <li>A financial compensation for your efforts.</li>
                </ul>
