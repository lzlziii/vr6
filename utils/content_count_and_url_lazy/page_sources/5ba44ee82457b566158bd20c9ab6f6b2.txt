<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><img class="size-full wp-image-3018 aligncenter" src="https://www.fireproofgames.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/FAQ_title.png" alt="" width="478" height="188"></strong><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><em><span style="color: #e3decf;"><strong>Where can I buy it &amp; how much will it cost?</strong></span></em> – The Room VR: A Dark Matter will be available to purchase from the Oculus Store, Steam and PlayStation Store for&nbsp;$29.99/€29.99.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><strong><em><span style="color: #e3decf;">What devices are compatible?</span></em> – </strong>The Room VR will be compatible with PlayStation VR, Oculus Meta Quest, Oculus Rift, Oculus Rift S, Valve Index, Vive Cosmos, HTC Vive, &amp; Windows Mixed Reality headsets.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><strong><em><span style="color: #e3decf;">What devices are not compatible?</span></em></strong> – The Room VR: A Dark Matter isn’t compatible with Oculus Go, Gear VR, Samsung VR, Google Cardboard, Nintendo Labo or any other mobile VR platform. This is simply because these platforms are not powerful enough to run the game.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><em><span style="color: #e3decf;"><strong>Can the game be played on mobile?</strong></span></em> – No. The Room VR: A Dark Matter is a VR exclusive title and can only be played by using a VR headset.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><strong><em><span style="color: #e3decf;">Are you giving up on mobile?</span></em> – </strong>After 8 years and four games in The Room series on mobile, we felt it was time to take a step back and try something new. We’ve always been big fans of VR, and the opportunity to bring The Room into virtual reality seemed like a great way to do that. That isn’t to say that we’ll never make another mobile Room game – we haven’t planned beyond the current game, and if we have a great idea for a Room game more suited to mobile then that’s definitely something we’d consider!</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><em><span style="color: #e3decf;"><strong>Why VR?</strong> </span></em>– We’ve always felt that the Room series would be incredibly well suited to VR, but it never felt like the right time to take that step. The release of more powerful and accessible headsets has finally allowed us to pursue this interest as a viable option.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #d4c9a7;"><strong><em><span style="color: #e3decf;">What is VR?</span></em> – </strong>VR stands for Virtual Reality. In order to play VR games, you need a VR headset + corresponding controller(s). The headset contains screens within it which allow you to see the gameplay – any head or hand movements carried out while wearing the headset will correlate to what you see within the headset. It can make for quite an immersive experience!</span></p>
