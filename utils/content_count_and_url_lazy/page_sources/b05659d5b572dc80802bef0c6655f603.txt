<div class="wb_cont_inner"><div id="wb_element_instance1" class="wb_element wb_text_element" data-plugin="TextArea" style=" line-height: normal;"><p class="wb-stl-custom1">&nbsp;</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">One day I decided to cycle my exercise bike while wearing an Oculus Go&nbsp;headset. Roller&nbsp;Coaster&nbsp;was the VR game especially suitable for my workouts.&nbsp;I bought a couple of similar VR games and started testing them. Gradually, I realized that such apps were not what I was looking for. The tracks were too short lasting only three or four minutes.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">As a result, I came up with my own cycling application. It lets you ride as long as you wish.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">The track is comfortable and calm being simultaneously diverse and captivating. To&nbsp;check the time, you always have a clock and a timer close at hand.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">I hope this application will motivate you to train and your exercise bike won’t turn into a&nbsp;clothes horse.<br>
I’ve been running this application for a long time and now I’m ready to offer it to you.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">Just get on your exercise bike wearing a VR headset and keep pedaling! You can ride without end along twisting lanes of a small island. The only limit is the battery capacity of your VR. You can use it with Oculus Quest and Quest2 now.&nbsp;</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">&nbsp;</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">Email for feedback: <em><strong>exrcsbike@gmail.com</strong></em></p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">&nbsp;</p>
</div><div id="wb_element_instance2" class="wb_element wb_text_element" data-plugin="TextArea" style=" line-height: normal;"><p class="wb-stl-custom1"><strong>The Tablet</strong></p>
</div><div id="wb_element_instance3" class="wb_element wb_text_element" data-plugin="TextArea" style=" line-height: normal;"><p class="wb-stl-custom1">If you look down, you can&nbsp;see a small tablet with some info.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">1. Current time from your Oculus Go/Quest.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">2. Activity timer.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">3. Technical information – frames per second&nbsp; and the velocity&nbsp;in arbitrary units.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">4. Lap counter.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">5. Local time on&nbsp;the island. One hour on the island&nbsp;is one minute in real time. A day on the&nbsp; island lasts 24 of our minutes.</p>
</div><div id="wb_element_instance4" class="wb_element wb_element_picture" data-plugin="Picture" title=""><img alt="" src="gallery_gen/224c525e5713dc9442bac6cce5203a66_452x266.58775510204.png" data-original-width="452" data-original-height="254"></div><div id="wb_element_instance5" class="wb_element wb_text_element" data-plugin="TextArea" style=" line-height: normal;"><p class="wb-stl-custom1">6. Current music track. There are five&nbsp;tracks in the application, each lasting&nbsp;about twenty minutes: “Casual Music”, “Fantasy Music”, “Asian Music”,&nbsp;“Medieval&nbsp;Music” and “Heavy&nbsp;Metal”. They are played in random order. However, you can turn off the sound of Oculus Go/Quest and listen to your favorite music, radio or podcasts from your smartphone.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1"><strong>The Oculus Go Controller</strong></p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">You can always interrupt the ride by pressing the «Back Button» of the Oculus Go controller, however I recommend that you use the «Oculus Button», since it works much more stably. Press the «Oculus Button» or the «Back Button» once shortly and the Oculus Interrupt dialog appears.</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1">&nbsp;</p>

<p class="wb-stl-custom1"><strong>The Oculus Quest&nbsp;Controller</strong><br>
You can always interrupt the ride by pressing the «Oculus Button» of the Oculus Quest touch controller. Press the «Oculus Button» once shortly and the Oculus Interrupt dialog appears.</p>
