		<div class="outter-wrap page2">
			<div class="inner">
				<h2>By Aviators, For Gamers</h2>
				<p>
					Acro FS is a recreational, stick-and-rudder flight simulator for high-performance aerobatic aircraft. 
					Practice your routine, race against the clock, or just get up there and fly - the checklist is done and the engine is running!
				</p>
				<p>
					Created by Jim Bourke, airshow pilot and member of the US Unlimited Aerobatic Team, Acro FS was designed from the ground up to accurately simulate aerobatic flight. 
					It allows you to experience what it’s like to strap into a stunt plane and take to the sky. 
				</p>
				<p>
					Hop into the cockpit, throttle up, and discover what you can do. 
					There’s no navigation, no radios - just a lot of horsepower in a state-of-the-art aircraft.
				</p>
			</div>

			</div>
		</div>
		
		<div class="outter-wrap page3">
			<div class="inner">
				<h2>Virtual Reality</h2>
				<p>
					For a truly immersive experience, use your Oculus, SteamVR, or Windows Mixed Reality headset. 
					Look around the cockpit, check your gauges, or take a glance out the wing to make sure that 45-degree upline is true.
				</p>
				<h2>Hyper-realism</h2>
				<p>
					Acro FS is built using Unigine2, a cutting-edge large-world game engine. 
					This gives the simulator tremendous realism, whether you’re looking down at the desert from 5000 feet or inspecting the instrument panel up close.
				</p>
				<h2>Racing</h2>
				<p>
					Test your skill against the clock, flying through inflatable pylons or holographic gates.
					Just be careful - the ground comes up fast!	
				</p>
				<h2>Robust Flight Model</h2>
				<p>
					Under the hood, Acro FS is powered by the <a href="https://www.knifeedge.com/cart/index.php?main_page=product_info&amp;products_id=181&amp;zenid=11i0j1ku2rpso2kp4eeghop3q5" target="_blank">FlightAxis</a> physics system. 
					This predictive model simulates each component of the aircraft and integrates these effects into a total response. 
					This allows Acro FS to model the exotic conditions created by aerobatic flight - there are no “blank spots” in the flight physics.
				</p>

				<p>
					Acro FS requires a controller to fly. For good control, you'll need a 4-axis controller.
