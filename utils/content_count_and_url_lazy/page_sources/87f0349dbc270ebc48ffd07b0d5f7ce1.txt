                    <h1>Virtual reality takes Parliament to the people</h1>

                    

    <nav aria-label="Breadcrumb">
        <ol class="nav breadcrumb">
                <li class="breadcrumb__item">
                    <a href="/en/" class="breadcrumb__link theme__link">

                    <div class="push--bottom-mobile">
                        


<a class="btn btn--float-v-align theme__btn alert__btn alert__btn-content" data-cy="alertButton" href="/en/alerts/maintenance/create-alert/content/27770">Get notifications</a>

                    <div class="publish-date">
                        <div><strong>Originally published:</strong> 1 November 2018</div>
                            <span><strong>Last updated:</strong> 1 November 2018</span>
                    </div>

                    	<div class="lede"><p>MPs had the opportunity to try out world-leading, Kiwi-created virtual reality experiences this week - and they’re keen to take them out to communities around New Zealand.</p></div>


                    <div class="body-text section--border-bottom-mobile push--top-mobile">
                        <div class="figure figure--image js-lightbox-single one-whole">
		<a href="/media/5277/vrlaunchmps8.jpg" class="figure__container js-lightbox-image" target="" data-caption="Deputy Speaker Hon Anne Tolley takes a virtual tour of Parliament" data-source="Office of the Clerk">
				<img src="/media/5277/vrlaunchmps8.EJ2TFg.jpg" data-original="/media/5277/vrlaunchmps8.EJ2TFg.jpg" alt="VRlaunchMPs8.jpg" class="js-lazy-auto " title="vrlaunchmps8" style="display: block;">
	<noscript>
		<img src="/media/5277/vrlaunchmps8.EJ2TFg.jpg" alt="VRlaunchMPs8.jpg" class="" title="vrlaunchmps8" />
	</noscript>

			<span class="figure__btn theme__btn">
					<span class="figure__btn-icon theme__text-inv iconf-zoom-in2" aria-hidden="true"></span>
					<span class="visuallyhidden">Enlarge image</span>
			</span>
		</a>

			<p class="figure__figcaption">
				Deputy Speaker Hon Anne Tolley takes a virtual tour of Parliament
			</p>
					<p class="figure__figcaption">
				Source: Office of the Clerk
			</p>
	</div>
<p>As the debating chamber turned 100, Parliament celebrated by launching its latest high-end, fully immersive virtual reality experience, the Virtual Debating Chamber. MPs also got to try its stable-mate, the <a href="https://www.parliament.nz/en/get-involved/features/take-a-spin-round-parliament-from-anywhere-in-the-country/">Parliament XR 360 tour</a>.</p>
<p>The experiences were created as part of Parliament’s commitment to engage with communities and encourage people to participate at Parliament.</p>
<p>Speaker Rt Hon Trevor Mallard explains why virtual reality is such an effective tool in this regard.</p>
<p>“MPs can be doing more to engage with communities. We want people to understand that MPs are around to represent them, and that Parliament is relevant to them,” he said.</p>
<p>“We want to engage in an innovative way - we understand that not everybody can come to Wellington and experience the place.”</p>
<p>Parliament is keen to engage with young people in particular, and virtual reality is designed to make exploring Parliament fun.</p>
<p>Mr Mallard continued by saying, “We want to use this opportunity to encourage young people in New Zealand to think about the range of exciting jobs that are available in digital industries”.</p>
<p><strong>Virtual Debating Chamber</strong></p>
<p>Developed for Parliament by local virtual reality experts PROJECTR® and Reality Virtual, using the latest virtual reality technology, the Virtual Debating Chamber builds on the Parliament XR app launched earlier this year. It takes immersive virtual experiences at Parliament to the next level, giving users the impression of walking around the chamber itself.</p>
<p>Users will feel like they’re standing on the signature green carpet and can reach out and touch the desks and the chairs. They’ll be able to focus on points of interest and learn more about the features of the debating chamber, including via video and sound bites that tie it to the lives of New Zealanders.</p>
<p>The Speaker plans to take the Virtual Debating Chamber out to schools and community groups.</p>
<p>“It’s designed to be part of a roadshow, to get out to community groups, libraries, other gatherings, and schools.”</p>
<p><strong>Parliament XR 360 tour</strong></p>
<p>The Parliament XR 360 virtual reality tour, Parliament XR, was launched at Mangere College earlier this year, and received lot of praise from year 9 and 10 students. <span><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-2fuByKU0FA">Find out what they had to say.</a></span></p>
<p>Parliament XR is available for free from the <span><a href="https://apple.co./2LiXCjS">App Store</a></span> and <span><a href="http://bit.ly/2zu3xzM">Google Play</a></span>. Spark is supporting the project by offering 10,000 free cardboard headsets to schools around the country to enhance the virtual reality experience for students.</p>
<p>If you’re a school or community group and would like a set of cardboard headsets for the Parliament XR app, just send an email to <span><a href="mailto:OOC.Education@parliament.govt.nz">OOC.Education@parliament.govt.nz</a></span>.</p>
